# IFRS 18 Presentation Pathfinder - Launch Plan

**Launch date:** Monday, 6 July 2026  
**Purpose:** Publish a factual, useful visual scoping resource alongside a LinkedIn post.

## Product promise

The Pathfinder helps a user see:

- how selected profit-or-loss lines could map into the five IFRS 18 categories;
- how operating profit, profit before financing and income taxes, and profit or loss could be affected;
- which disclosure and transition workstreams may be relevant;
- where management judgement or further evidence is required; and
- when a detailed IFRS 18 impact assessment would be valuable.

It is not a compliance engine, accounting opinion, disclosure checklist or substitute for entity-specific analysis.

## Launch scope

### 1. Technical accuracy and currency

- Reflect the June 2026 IAS 28 fair-value-option amendment.
- Distinguish equity-accounted associate/JV results from eligible fair-value returns.
- Correct amended IAS 7 wording for interest and dividend cash flows.
- Apply the IFRS 18 cash-and-cash-equivalents logic at a suitable scoping level.
- Retain the illustrative financing-liability policy and subtotal treatment, with explicit assumptions.
- Add IFRS paragraph and implementation-source references to classification outputs.

### 2. Clear visual scoping

- Describe outputs as indicative outcomes based on selected assumptions.
- Retain rule-based and judgement labels.
- Use all five IFRS 18 category descriptions consistently.
- Rename the pre-mapping comparison so it does not imply the entity's current IAS 1 presentation has been reconstructed.
- Add sign warnings for unusual income and expense inputs.

### 3. Guided disclosure and transition previews

- Provide suggested disclosure wording as a drafting starting point.
- Show a specified-expenses allocation preview and any unallocated totals.
- Provide an MPM screening helper, required disclosure components and illustrative wording.
- Show a transition mapping preview and explain the IFRS 18.C3 reconciliation requirement.
- Include evidence prompts and questions a finance team, adviser or auditor is likely to ask.

These previews explain the work required; they do not prepare a complete compliant note or working paper.

### 4. Market positioning and conversion

- Add a visible "scope, not compliance" label.
- Add a results-page call to action encouraging users not to wait for the year-end audit.
- Link the call to action to Gerrit Heyneke's LinkedIn profile.
- Keep the local-only privacy model prominent.

### 5. Launch quality controls

- Replace regression tests that validate superseded or over-broad assumptions.
- Add fixtures for equity-accounted and fair-value associate/JV results, cash equivalents, IAS 7 wording, five-category copy and sign warnings.
- Test all 14 profiles, JSON restore, CSV export, print output, desktop and mobile layouts.
- Update the README and rules-pack version.

## Deferred beyond launch

- A complete editable paragraph 83 allocation workpaper.
- A full MPM governance and approval workflow.
- User overrides, reviewer sign-off and audit-status controls.
- A complete IFRS 18.C3 reconciliation builder.
- Recognition, measurement, consolidation or reporting-framework conclusions.

The launch version still explains and previews each of these areas where relevant.
